Price blog week 8

Create some hypothesis about the price of your product.

Pricing - Louis

Cost based pricing is a simple way of pricing, which consists of adding a profit margin to the cost of the product.

The simplicity of application is a great advantage and the guarantee to make money. For these two reasons, this is usually how an e-commerce business sets its prices while taking its first steps into the digital world.

Estimated expenses
Expenses Prices (kr/month)
Salaries 45000
Office 15000
Hardware 600
Internet 500
Heat/electricity 300
Insurance 1000
Marketing 15000
Hosting + domain name 750
Total 78150

We know this are low salaries, but we chose not to give ourselves large salaries to limit costs as much as possible. We are 3 co-founders, so it represents \(15.000\) kr. per person per month. The next cost covers the office rent, but also everything related to it as cleaning, furniture and of course coffee. About the hardware part that is normally not a monthly cost, we chose to spread the cost over 3 years. This money will be used to have 3 corrects setups to work in good conditions. Other expenses are basic as internet, electricity, insurance, marketing and hosting for our application. We found that all these expenses represent \(78,150\) kr. per month.

Based on this, we had to choose a consistent price because this one will give us the minimum number of customers necessary for us to be in the positive. With a price that is too low, we would need a lot of customers who convert to the premium option to be profitable. With a price that is too high, we would need fewer customers, but maybe they will be more reluctant to subscribe to our premium option.

Moreover, we also based on the value that our product can represent for the customers. Indeed, we will clearly highlight the money saved thanks to our application and show it to users. Therefore, a person with proof that they can save \(400\) kr. with our product is more likely to subscribe. Then we priced our solution \(90\) kr. per month.

On we have a graph. The red line represents the evolution of sales in relation to the number of customers, and the green line represents expenses. As we can see with this price, our solution will be profitable with \(869\) customers that subscribe to the premium version.
