ODI model

The principle of Outcome Driven Innovation (ODI) is that people buy products and services to satisfy a certain purpose, not for the product or service itself. Thus, instead of spending time improving those, the goal is to focus on the customer’s primary need, rather than on the solution they are using. The focus is clearly on the "what", not the "how".

The Outcome Driven Innovation method was designed by Anthony Ulwick. The goal of ODI is to find new opportunities for products and services that customers truly need and can no longer do without.

On we can observe the different steps which we must go through to apply Anthony Ulwick’s model. In the following part we will start with the definition of the targeted customers, followed by the jobs-to-be-done that corresponds to the objectives that our product will have to fulfill. Then we are going to highlight uncovered and unmet needs of existing solutions, and we will end in this part with the opportunities.
