Chasm & Blitzscaling blog week 11

Crossing the chasm and bowling alley strategy

Afterwards, we also want to target students because we have been in this situation and we know that it can sometimes be financially complicated, so you must know how to moderate your expenses and manage your budget. Moreover, since the students are young, we believe that they will be the most likely to use our product.

It could be also a product useful for families (especially with several children), because you have a lot of different expenses and you can easily be lost. Our product can help them to have more visualization on their budgets.

Blitzscaling

Since is currently in stage \(1\) i.e. a Family we are able to move quickly and smooth and the time from idea to action is short. This means we are able to react and move quickly, can prioritise speed in cost of efficiency, in an uncertain environment; the precise definition of Blitzscaling!

There are 4 different growth factors when Blitzscaling. The first is market size and we have in shown the initial size of the market when starting our blitzscale. The total available market (TAM) is described in [sec:market_size]. The distribution of will not be a problem since we are not producing/storing/distributing any physical products only software.

Since the price for AWS Lambda functions is low (\(35.8\$\) for \(10,000,000\) requests). We can more concretely handle a lot of traffic at a low cost. Meaning that with the price model described in [sec:pricing], we are able to have high gross margins.

The last growth factor is network effects, where the product gets better the more users are on it/using. It will not directly help the users like a new user to TikTok1. But it will contribute the underlying algorithms and create better expense plans since it will be based on more data.

These 4 growth factors all align with Peter Thiel’s view on how to build a monopoly since the growth factors are approximately the same as the characteristics of a monopoly. We want to start small and monopolize in the F.I.R.E. space since it is easier to dominate a small market then a large one; "if you think your market to big in the beginning it properly is"2.
