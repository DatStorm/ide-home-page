Monopoly blog week 7

If you were to create a monopoly using one of the four methods discussed. How would you do it?

Possibility for a Monopoly

We have assessed the possibility to achieve a monopoly in our space. We have evaluated this on the four criteria shown in the lecture of week 7. summarizes the results.

Personal spending and income-tracking is a crowded space, but to our knowledge none of the existing solutions really use detailed receipt data. A detailed categorisation of all receipt items into a taxonomy will set us apart from the rest. The degree to which this is a big technological advantage is unclear though. Our current method of categorisation is to do it by hand, and based on our own experience we have estimated one can sufficiently categorise up to 100 items per hour. Thus if others were to go into the space of receipt-tracking, categorising tens of thousands of items is not a big barrier of entry.

For network effects we have considered the advantage of having data from a large number of different users. It has been shown again and again that data is extremely valuable and an important strategic asset. This is the case for all the big tech companies like Google, Facebook, Amazon etc who have built their business up around data collection. We suspect gathering data can directly help the quality of our product. When it comes to creating useful and actionable savings plans, a large amount of data can be used to detect where individuals are outliers in their spending habits. User data can also be used to track whether or not savings plans we propose are actually followed through and from that guide how our algorithms should be constructed. The degree to which this actually improves the end product is quite difficult to assess however.

Since we primarily are a software company with no physical goods it is not obvious how economics of scale will help.

Given that technologically our product is not particularly difficult to build nor has a high barrier of entry, branding might be the best way to a monopoly. If we through proper branding can position ourselves in the minds of people as the way to cut expenses and save on bad habits it might be possible to establish some sort of monopoly. Hopefully we could be the first to market with this particular categorisation-based approach to savings and start by getting a strong foothold with the most money-conscious people. With this we could build an image of being uniquely focused and competent at savings.

Anders Stormer
