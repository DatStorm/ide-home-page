BMC blog week 6

Create a business model canvas for your company.

Business Model Canvas

We want to understand how we serve our customers, what value propositions we offer them and through what channels we provide value. We also want to look at how our company makes money. To get a structured overview we chose to use the tool Business Model Canvas (BMC) seen for in [fig:bmc].

The BMC starts with first trying to name the purpose of the business i.e. what value are we trying to deliver to the customer. Our value proposition is saving money first of all and doing that in smarter ways, then before to get the job done. This means our customer can perform better by getting a greater overview and based on this can take deliberate actions.

Customer Relationships

We had different hypothesis for our relationship with our customer. Should be an automated service or dedicated personal assistance such that you could chat/call with a financial expert a bit like "Luksusfælden"1. This person would look over your finances make a budget based on your needs and present it to you. Follow ups would be made each month to tweek the budget and verify the progress. We found out though that the price for this would exceed what most people are willing to pay. So we looked further into automated service or software as a service (SAAS). The products in this category are rising so we those this direction.

This means the relation with our customer becomes virtual and almost non existent, meaning the product has to be awesome and should keep the user engaged in using our product.

Key Partners, Key Activities & Key Resources

These three boxes describe how works behind the scenes - what operational components are we doing to make our value proposition a reality. In this paragraph we list all vital ingredients and invaluable allies that make sure our business exist.

Key Partners are Storebox. They are the back-end of the infrastructure. They make the receipt collection and storage of our front-end product. To sign up for there service, the customers they will most likely use MobilePay. The only other supplier we need are somewhere to host our service an obvious choice would be to host on Amazon Lambda2.

Key Activities are problem solving, more concretely how to make sure the user does save money. Are they making progress or are we at least trying our best to help them make progress, we have to make sure that since we are a software company that our platform as a service, is the best it can be.

Key Resources are the algorithms used to create the expense budget for the customer. We are independent of place, machines, patents and other physical resources since we are not responsible for data storage and gathering. So most of our resources is software and free online services.

Cost Structure & Revenue Streams

Our structure is cost driven meaning we want maximum automation and the value we create should be for the customer as much as possible by utilising before mentioned software services. More concretely our most expensive record is running the service at the moment. The revenue comes from the freemium model, as described earlier, meaning the basic plan is free but the premium plan is paid. An in depth discussion of this is made in [sec:pricing]. The hypothesis for how we priced our products what the cost is and our revenue streams was made in depth in Section [sec:pricing].
